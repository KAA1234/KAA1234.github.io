What is Encryption? Encryption is the process of encoding data in order to make it unreadable. Encryption provides confidentiality of data. Data can be encrypted in each of its three states; at rest, in motion and in use. [CIA Triangle, 3 states] The Threat This blog post focuses on data in motion specific to websites. Without encryption data in motion could be intercepted by an attacker revealing sensitive information such as passwords and credit card data. Attackers can intercept data through various techniques such as ARP attacks and passive network monitoring via taps, port mirroring and wifi sniffers. [Man in the middle] HTTP vs HTTPs Website communication typically happen either over HTTP (Port 80) or HTTPS (Port 443). HTTP stands for Hyper Text Markup Language (HTTP) and is unencrypted. During the early days of the internet all website data was sent and received over HTTP. This was a big problem as all of the data was sent in plaintext and vulnerable to attackers.
